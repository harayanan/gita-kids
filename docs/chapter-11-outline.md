# Chapter 11 — Vishvarupa Darshana Yoga (The Yoga of the Cosmic Vision)

## Overview
- **Verses:** 55
- **Folk Art Style:** Madhubani (Mithila, Bihar)
- **Central Theme:** Arjuna asks to see Krishna's universal form. Krishna grants him a divine eye, and Arjuna beholds the **Vishvarupa** — the whole universe, all gods, all worlds, all time, blazing in one infinite body with countless faces and arms. Overwhelmed and terrified (he sees the warriors rushing into fiery mouths), Arjuna praises and begs forgiveness, then asks for the gentle form back. Krishna reassures him and shows His familiar shape, teaching that this cosmic form is seen not by ritual or study but by **undivided love** (11.54), and closing with the call to do His work, free of attachment and enmity (11.55).

## Philosophical Orientation
Advaita Vedanta. The Vishvarupa shows the One appearing as the all — every god, world, and being is within the single divine reality; Krishna is Time/Brahman, source and dissolver of all (11.32). The vision's lesson: the same Lord is everything, reached by love (11.54). Handle the terrifying imagery (mouths, destruction) with awe but child-appropriate care — wonder, not horror. Krishna's reassurance (11.49–51) is tender; emphasise comfort after awe.

## Recurring Modern Characters (Madhubani / Mithila, Bihar)
- **Ravi** — a 9-year-old Mithila boy (from Ch6), with his puppy **Moti**.
- **Nani (Sarita)** — his grandmother, a Madhubani painter.
- Use sparingly — this chapter is one continuous cosmic vision, so it is mostly mythological. Modern stories fit only the calm framing/teaching/emotional verses.

## Story Mix
~85% mythological (≈47), ~15% modern (≈8: verses **5, 8, 24, 49, 51, 53, 54, 55**). The vision verses (9–31, 36–46) depict the actual cosmic form (Madhubani: many-faced, many-armed luminous Krishna filling the frame with suns, gods, worlds, patterned fills) — keep them mythological. Modern stories fit the awe-of-vastness (a child under the Mithila night sky), the comfort-after-fear, and the closing teaching on love. Vary mythological framing — the chariot, the granting of the divine eye, the gods watching, the warriors, the gentle form restored. Do not reuse a beat.
