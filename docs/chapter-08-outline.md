# Chapter 8 — Akshara Brahma Yoga (The Yoga of the Eternal Spirit)

## Overview
- **Verses:** 28
- **Folk Art Style:** Pattachitra (Odisha)
- **Central Theme:** What happens at the moment of death, and how remembering God carries us beyond. Krishna answers Arjuna's seven questions (Brahman, the Self, action, the physical and divine realms, the Lord of sacrifice, and how He is known at death), teaches that "whatever you remember at the last moment, that you become," and so to remember Him always. He reveals the imperishable syllable **Om**, the vast day-and-night cycles of Brahma, the eternal Unmanifest beyond the perishable one, and the two paths (bright and dark) the departed travel.

## Philosophical Orientation
Advaita Vedanta. The Akshara (Imperishable) = Brahman = Krishna's supreme nature; the Atman is Brahman; Om is the sound-symbol of Brahman; remembrance (smarana) at death works because the mind rests in the Self. Krishna speaks AS Brahman. Treat death gently and without fear — as a doorway, a remembering, not an ending. Keep it warm and child-safe.

## Recurring Modern Characters (Pattachitra / Odisha)
- **Aarav** — 11-year-old boy in Puri (from Ch3/Ch13).
- **Dadu** — his grandfather, retired fisherman and gentle philosopher; well-suited to calm talk about endings, remembering, and the eternal sea.
- Vary with **Hari Uncle** (farmer), a Pattachitra painter in Raghurajpur, and the sea/cycles of tide as imagery. Avoid over-using one pair (earlier QA flag).

## Story Mix
~70% mythological (≈20), ~30% modern (≈8: verses **6, 7, 12, 14, 17, 19, 24, 27**). Vary mythological sources — death-and-remembrance tales (Ajamila who cried "Narayana," King Bharata reborn as a deer because he thought of his deer at death, King Khatvanga who had one hour to live, Bhishma awaiting the northern solstice to die), the two paths (Chandogya/Katha Upanishad, Nachiketa), the cosmic day-and-night of Brahma (Markandeya), and the syllable Om. Do not reuse a tale.
