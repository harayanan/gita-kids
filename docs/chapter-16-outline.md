# Chapter 16 — Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga (The Yoga of Divine and Demonic Qualities)

## Overview
- **Verses:** 24
- **Folk Art Style:** Madhubani (Mithila, Bihar)
- **Central Theme:** Two kinds of character a person can grow: the **divine** (daivi) — fearlessness, purity, kindness, truth, generosity, calm, humility — which leads to freedom; and the **demonic** (asuri) — pride, harshness, greed, anger, conceit — which leads to bondage and sorrow. Krishna lists both, describes how the selfish mind thinks and traps itself, names the **three gates of darkness — desire (kāma), anger (krodha), greed (lobha)** — and says to drop them and let wise guidance (the scriptures) light the way.

## Speaker Map (CRITICAL)
- **Krishna:** ALL 24 verses. Every `speaker` is `krishna`.

## Philosophical Orientation
Advaita Vedanta, gently applied to character. "Divine" and "demonic" are tendencies in everyone, not separate races of beings — and Krishna reassures Arjuna he is born to the divine (16.5). Treat the demonic descriptions as the unkind, grasping way of living (habits to outgrow), NOT as frightening monsters. Emphasise hope: anyone can drop the three gates and turn toward the light. Child-safe, never scary; the redemptive note dominates.

## Recurring Modern Characters (Madhubani / Mithila, Bihar)
- **Ravi** (Mithila boy, puppy Moti) and **Nani (Sarita)**, the Madhubani painter, from Ch6/Ch11.
- Vary with village neighbours who show kindness vs selfishness in small, concrete ways.

## Story Mix
~70% mythological (≈17), ~30% modern (≈7: verses **1, 3, 5, 13, 21, 22, 24**). Best mythological anchor: **Prahlada (divine child) vs Hiranyakashipu (demonic father)** — one family showing both natures (use across several demonic-nature verses without repeating the same beat); also **Ravana's ego/greed**, the devas-and-asuras contrast, and a sage embodying the divine list.
