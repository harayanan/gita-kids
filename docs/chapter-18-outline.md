# Chapter 18 — Moksha Sannyasa Yoga (The Yoga of Liberation)

## Overview
- **Verses:** 78 (the longest chapter; the Gita's grand summary)
- **Folk Art Style:** Pattachitra (Odisha)
- **Central Theme:** Krishna gathers the whole teaching. The difference between **sannyasa** (renouncing selfish action) and **tyaga** (giving up the fruits of action); how knowledge, action, the doer, intellect, firmness, and happiness each come in three guna-colours; the value of doing one's own duty (**svadharma**) offered to God; and the supreme secret — **surrender wholly to Me and I will free you from all sorrow** (18.66). It closes with Arjuna's doubts gone (18.73) and Sanjaya's joyful epilogue (18.74–78): "wherever there is Krishna and Arjuna, there is fortune and victory."

## Speaker Map (CRITICAL)
- **Arjuna:** verses 1 and 73.
- **Krishna:** verses 2–72.
- **Sanjaya:** verses 74–78 (his closing words to King Dhritarashtra).
- `speaker`: arjuna for 1 & 73; krishna for 2–72; sanjaya for 74–78.

## Philosophical Orientation
Advaita Vedanta brought to its culmination: action offered to God without attachment purifies and leads to knowledge; the Self is not the doer (the five factors + the gunas act, 18.13–16); realising Brahman one gains supreme devotion and enters the Lord (18.54–55); total surrender (sharanagati, 18.62, 18.66) is the final secret. Krishna is the Self in all hearts (18.61). Keep the long guna-triads concrete and child-friendly; let the climax feel like a warm homecoming, not a lecture.

## Recurring Modern Characters (Pattachitra / Odisha)
- **Aarav** (Puri boy), **Dadu** (grandfather, retired fisherman/philosopher), **Hari Uncle** (rice farmer) — from Ch3/Ch8/Ch13.
- The svadharma verses suit "everyone has their own work" stories; the surrender verses suit warm trust stories.

## Story Mix
~70% mythological (≈55), ~30% modern (≈23). Suggested modern verses: **9, 11, 14, 20, 23, 26, 30, 33, 36, 37, 42, 44, 45, 47, 48, 58, 60, 61, 63, 65, 66, 71, 72**. Vary mythological sources widely — Krishna's lila, the Mahabharata frame (Arjuna's resolve, the chariot), sages of renunciation, the four duties via different figures (a brahmin sage, a kshatriya like Janaka/Rama, a vaishya, a devoted servant), the surrender of Draupadi/Gajendra (18.66), Sanjaya & Vyasa (epilogue). Do not reuse a beat. Keep the final verses (74–78) on the battlefield/chariot wonder.
